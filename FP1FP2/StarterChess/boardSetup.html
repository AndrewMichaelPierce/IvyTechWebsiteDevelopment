Setting up the Chessboard.

Starting a game of chess begins with the placement of the board and the setup of the pieces. While quite easy, this simple step is often incorrectly performed by beginners and can result in uneven play since the setup they use can change subtlety each time.

The chessboard

  • The lightest colored square will be in each players bottom right corner.
  • This is often remembered by the phrase, "White to your right."

Piece Setup

  • The eight pawns sit on each respective player's second row away from them.
  • From the corners on the first row sit the rook, knight and bishop, in that order.
  • With the last two squares in the middle sit the queen and king.
  • The queen always sits on "her color."
  • This means the light colored queen sits on the lightest colored square.
  • The darkest colored queen sits on the darkest colored square.
  • The king sits on the leftover square.
  • The kings and queens do sit opposite of their respective piece on the same file
